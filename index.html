United States held a 59th presidential election on Tuesday November 3rd 2020. With a record high voter turnout since 120 years, Joe Biden won aganist Donald Trump. Trump became the first president to lose re-election since George H.W. Bush in 1992. 538 electoral votes with 270 required for majority, Biden carried 306 votes while trump carried 232 votes. That result is the exact opposite of 2016 result if not for faithless electors.

Each 50 states and Washington DC have its rules and laws differently, including Nebraska and Maine which awards electoral votes by congressional district instead of winner takes all. 1 electoral vote awarded for each congressional district, and 2 remaining electoral votes for statewide. In Nebraska, Trump won statewide, 1st and 3rd congressional district with 4 electoral votes, while Biden won 2nd congressional district with 1 electoral vote. In Maine, Biden won statewide and 1st congressional district with 3 electoral votes, while Trump won 2nd congressional district with 1 electoral vote.
